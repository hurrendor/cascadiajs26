## Abstract: 

What if you owned your social media data and could move freely between apps? ATProto makes this possible. I'll teach the fundamentals—DIDs, data repos, why they're important, and how to build your first decentralized social platform.
## Community talk notes: 

### Scribe: Mike Wasserman
- how we got here: early internet fostering human connection
  - monetization changed the focus of apps: connect, grow, advertise, maximize profits
  - app platforms use design to trap uses, a/b testing, infinte rescroll, pull to refresh
  - large platforms own your data, they become hard to leave (connections and data are there), they also close (G+, myspace, twitter)
- ATProto (the atmosphere): you own your data, move likes posts, and follows on to open web
  - If an app does something you don't like, you can just leave without losing anything
  - You own your data, you can delete/edit??!?
- How it works: Each user gets their own repo where their data is stored
  - User has decentralized id and a handle (like dns)
  - Records as JSON data (post or likes), blobs (like images)
- Show data on bluesky; now anyone can create another app (a Bluesky competitor) to read that same data
  - Blacksky, Witchsky, Anisota
  - each brings their own features
- AtStore: apps on the atmosphere, examples:
  - Sifa ID: professional id network, your own data hosts your history, skills, etc.
  - Streamplace: OSS live streaming app
  - rpg.actor: role playing characters for tabletop and other games
  - atmo.quest: share your qr code to connect and write followup notes
- platform where identity and auth layer are already solved; good community
