## Abstract: 

AI, React, Vue - what do all of these things have in common? They are tools meant to enhance the development experience - we must remember that. Join me in going over best practices on using AI to LEARN and not to do you your job for you “poorly I might add”. I'll take you through the steps of how I used AI as a React developer to learn Vue - did I actually learn? Join this talk to find out!
## Community talk notes: 

### Why think about using AI to learn?
AI is a skill to be learned. Consider it as the Ultimate Rubber Ducky

The Learning Loop for languages
1. Translate the fundamentals of what you know to the concept you're trying to learn
   - Understand why - literally, ask "why?"
   - Translate and summarize the concepts
2. Practice / Review
   - Simulate debugging
   - Feynman Technique
     - Explain your understanding of the concept to AI, and confirm it's correct
3. Convert
   - Use what you've learned to translate from what you know. In code, building the same piece of code in the alternate language (IE, React --> Vue)
4. Refactor together
5. Apply what you've learned

Pro tip: Always say "thank you" :)
